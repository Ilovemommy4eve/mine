CAMPING TRIP IN MOUNT COOK NATIONAL PARK, NEW ZEALAND

New Zealand is a country known for its breathtaking natural landscapes and outdoor adventure opportunities. One of the most popular destinations for nature lovers and adventure seekers is Mount Cook National Park. Located in the South Island of New Zealand, this national park boasts stunning alpine scenery, glaciers, and hiking trails that attract visitors from all over the world.

Day 1 : Arrival and Set-Up

We arrived at the national park early in the morning and set up our campsite at the White Horse Hill Campground. The campground was located at the base of Mount Cook, providing stunning views of the mountain and the surrounding valley. We spent the day exploring the nearby trails and soaking in the natural beauty of the area.

Day 3 :Exploring Tasman Glacier

On the third day, we took a guided tour to explore the Tasman Glacier. The tour included a boat ride on the glacier lake and a hike on the glacier itself. Seeing the glacier up close was a surreal experience, and we were amazed by the sheer size and power of the ice formations.
